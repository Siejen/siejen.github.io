Siejen's Experience

Siejen holds an MBA and B.S. in Management with a dual concentration in Finance and Marketing from Binghamton University along with a Certificate in International Business and Finance from New York University. After spending the first eight years of her career in the glamorous fashion industry on 7th Avenue in New York City managing all facets of the trade from merchandising to product licensing to product development, Siejen relocated to the San Francisco Bay Area in 2008 and is currently the Assistant Director of the US-Asia Technology Management Center at Stanford University.

Siejen serves as the Marketing Chair on the Executive Committee of the MIT-Stanford VLAB organization and is also a Provisional Member of the Junior League of Palo Alto-Mid Peninsula. Most recently, Siejen was a Fellow of the Leadership Palo Alto Class of 2013, and she is a judge for the 2014 Palo Alto Apps Challenge. Formerly a Board Member of the Association of Women MBAs, Siejen was also the Vice-President of Events for the Future Women Leaders organization where she was involved managing 40+ events and programs over a 2 year time period including the launch of the new Conversations with the Chief program designed to connect members with C-Suite level executives.
